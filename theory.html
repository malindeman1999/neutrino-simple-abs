State and Measurement Vectors

Define a small-signal state vector around the operating point:

\[ \mathbf{x}(\omega) = \begin{bmatrix} \delta T(\omega) \\ \delta x(\omega) \\ \delta y(\omega) \end{bmatrix} \quad x \equiv \frac{f-f_0}{f_0},\; y \equiv \frac{1}{2Q_i} \]

Measured baseband quadratures are:

\[ \mathbf{m}(\omega)= \begin{bmatrix} \delta I(\omega) \\ \delta Q(\omega) \end{bmatrix} \]

Linearized Forward Model

Near resonance, the readout is linearized as:

\[ \mathbf{m}(\omega)=\mathbf{R}(\omega)\,\mathbf{x}(\omega)+\mathbf{n}_{amp}(\omega) \]

with Jacobian

\[ \mathbf{R}(\omega)= \begin{bmatrix} \partial I/\partial T & \partial I/\partial x & \partial I/\partial y \\ \partial Q/\partial T & \partial Q/\partial x & \partial Q/\partial y \end{bmatrix} \]

The thermal-to-resonator chain can be factored as:

\[ \mathbf{x}(\omega)=\mathbf{H}(\omega)\,\mathbf{u}(\omega) \]

Use the source vector

\[ \mathbf{u}(\omega)= \begin{bmatrix} p_E(\omega)\\ p_{TFN}(\omega)\\ n_{TLS}(\omega)\\ n_{GR}(\omega)\\ \delta T_b(\omega) \end{bmatrix} \]

with explicit transfer matrix

\[ \mathbf{H}(\omega)= \begin{bmatrix} \dfrac{1}{C(1+i\omega\tau_{th})} & \dfrac{1}{C(1+i\omega\tau_{th})} & 0 & 0 & \dfrac{G/C}{1+i\omega\tau_{th}}\\[1.1em] \dfrac{k_T}{1+i\omega\tau_{res}} & \dfrac{k_T}{1+i\omega\tau_{res}} & k_{x,TLS} & k_{x,GR} & \dfrac{k_T\,G/C}{1+i\omega\tau_{res}}\\[1.1em] \dfrac{\ell_T}{1+i\omega\tau_{res}} & \dfrac{\ell_T}{1+i\omega\tau_{res}} & k_{y,TLS} & k_{y,GR} & \dfrac{\ell_T\,G/C}{1+i\omega\tau_{res}} \end{bmatrix} \]

This writes thermal and resonator poles explicitly while allowing direct TLS/GR injection into frequency-like and dissipation-like channels.

Definitions of \( \alpha \) and \( \beta \) (Impedance Form)

Let the KID complex impedance be \(Z(T,I)=Z'(T,I)+iZ''(T,I)\).

Your thermal coefficients are split between phase and amplitude channels and are defined from \(dZ/dT\):

\[ \alpha_{\phi}\equiv \frac{1}{2}\,\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial T}, \qquad \alpha_{A}\equiv \frac{1}{2}\,\frac{\partial \ln A}{\partial T}, \]

with \(\phi=\arg Z\) and \(A=|Z|\). Equivalently, using \(Z\):

\[ \frac{\partial \ln A}{\partial T}=\Re\!\left(\frac{1}{Z}\frac{\partial Z}{\partial T}\right), \qquad \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial T}=\Im\!\left(\frac{1}{Z}\frac{\partial Z}{\partial T}\right). \]

Your current-response coefficients are the \(\beta\) terms from \(dZ/dI\):

\[ \beta_{\phi}\equiv \frac{1}{2}\,\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial I}, \qquad \beta_{A}\equiv \frac{1}{2}\,\frac{\partial \ln A}{\partial I}, \]

or equivalently

\[ \frac{\partial \ln A}{\partial I}=\Re\!\left(\frac{1}{Z}\frac{\partial Z}{\partial I}\right), \qquad \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial I}=\Im\!\left(\frac{1}{Z}\frac{\partial Z}{\partial I}\right). \]

Noise PSD Matrix \( \mathbf{S} \) and Mapping to I/Q

Combine the blocks:

\[ \mathbf{m}(\omega)=\underbrace{\mathbf{R}(\omega)\mathbf{H}(\omega)}_{\mathbf{G}(\omega)}\,\mathbf{u}(\omega)+\mathbf{n}_{amp}(\omega) \]

so \(\mathbf{G}(\omega)\) is the matrix that maps each physical noise source into \(I\) and \(Q\).

Define your source cross-spectral density matrix as

\[ \mathbf{S}(\omega)\equiv\mathbf{S}_u(\omega)= \begin{bmatrix} S_{EE} & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & S_{TFN} & 0 & 0 & S_{TFN,b}\\ 0 & 0 & S_{TLS} & S_{TLS,GR} & 0\\ 0 & 0 & S_{GR,TLS} & S_{GR} & 0\\ 0 & S_{b,TFN} & 0 & 0 & S_{bb} \end{bmatrix} \]

(set off-diagonal terms to zero if you assume independent sources).

A common explicit TLS term is

\[ S_{TLS}(f)=A_{TLS}\,f^{-\beta}, \]

which is inserted in the \((3,3)\) element of \(\mathbf{S}(\omega)\) (and optionally correlated terms).

Then with amplifier/readout noise \(\mathbf{S}_{amp}(\omega)\):

\[ \mathbf{S}_{IQ}(\omega)=\mathbf{G}(\omega)\,\mathbf{S}(\omega)\,\mathbf{G}^{\dagger}(\omega)+\mathbf{S}_{amp}(\omega) \]

Here \(\mathbf{S}_{IQ}\) has entries \(S_{II},S_{QQ},S_{IQ}\), including cross-correlation.

Interpretation for This TKID Calorimeter

  • The \(\tau_{qp} \ll \tau_{res} \ll \tau_{th}\) hierarchy keeps the resonator in quasi-static thermal tracking, so \(\mathbf{R}(\omega)\) stays well-conditioned over the signal band.
  • TLS noise enters mainly through the frequency-like quadrature direction and appears in \(\mathbf{S}_u\) or as an additive term in \(\mathbf{S}_{IQ}\).
  • Thermal fluctuation noise is set by \(C\) and \(G\), then projected into \(I/Q\) by \(\partial I/\partial T,\partial Q/\partial T\).
  • Optimal filtering and energy-estimator equations are documented on the Signal Processing page.
