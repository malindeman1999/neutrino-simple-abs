Critical Timescale Hierarchy

Required ordering:

\(\tau_{qp} \ll \tau_{res} \ll \tau_{th}\)

QuantityMeaningTarget
\(\tau_{qp}\)Quasiparticle lifetime\(< 1\,\mu s\)
\(\tau_{res}\)Resonator response time\(1{-}2\,\mu s\)
\(\tau_{th}\)Thermal decay time\(5{-}10\,\mu s\)

Resonator Dynamics

\(\tau_{res} = \dfrac{Q}{\pi f_0}\)

Constraint: \(\tau_{res} \le \tau_{th}/3\)

Implications:

  • Q cannot be arbitrarily high.
  • \(f_0\) must remain in the GHz band.
  • Resonator bandwidth must preserve pulse shape.

Thermal Physics

\(\tau_{th} = C/G\)

For \(\tau_{th} \approx 5{-}8\,\mu s\), required conductance is \(G \approx 1\times10^{-10}\,\mathrm{W/K}\).

Coupling hierarchy requirement:

\(G_{AK} \ge 10\,G_{AB}\) and therefore \(G_{AK} \gg G_{AB}\).

This enforces single-node thermal behavior and suppresses internal thermal fluctuation noise.

Energy Resolution Model

Baseline relation:

\(\Delta E \approx \sqrt{k_B T^2 C}\)

With responsivity alpha:

\(\Delta E \propto \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{\alpha}}\sqrt{k_B T^2 C}\)

CaseExpected DeltaE
Ideal3-5 meV
Realistic20-100 meV

TLS Frequency Noise

TLS contribution scaling:

\(\dfrac{S_f}{f^2} \propto \dfrac{F\,\tan(\delta_{TLS})}{f^{\beta}}\)

TLS depends on capacitor geometry and dielectric quality, not on thermal design.

Design condition at \(100{-}200\,\mathrm{kHz}\): keep fractional frequency noise below \(\sim 10^{-18}\).
