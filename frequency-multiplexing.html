Assumptions

ParameterValue
Project baseline center frequency\(f_c = 1.0\,\mathrm{GHz}\)
Resonators per chip1000
Fractional spacing \(\Delta f / f = 0.002\) (0.2%)

Bandwidth per Chip

Approximate occupied fractional bandwidth:

\[ \frac{B}{f_c} \approx N\,\frac{\Delta f}{f} =1000\times0.002=2. \]

So the chip requires a total bandwidth of about:

\[ B\approx 2f_c. \]

Examples:

Center frequency \(f_c\)Required \(B\)Example span
1.0 GHz~2.0 GHz0.2-2.2 GHz
2.0 GHz~4.0 GHz0.5-4.5 GHz

This shows why fixed 2-5 GHz is not mandatory: lower-frequency plans are viable if the readout chain supports the required absolute bandwidth.

Constraint with Resonator Time Constant

Frequency choice must still satisfy:

\[ \tau_{res}=\frac{Q}{\pi f_0} \le \tau_{th}/3. \]

Lower \(f_0\) can be used by correspondingly lowering \(Q\) (or using faster thermal design), while preserving pulse fidelity and multiplexing margin.

Practical Recommendation

Project assumption: use the 1 GHz center-frequency case (\(\sim 0.2{-}2.2\,\mathrm{GHz}\) span) for current design sizing.

Define final band from three coupled requirements:

  1. 1000 resonators/chip at \(\Delta f/f=0.002\)
  2. Readout electronics instantaneous bandwidth and linearity
  3. Timing rule \(\tau_{res}\ll\tau_{th}\)
