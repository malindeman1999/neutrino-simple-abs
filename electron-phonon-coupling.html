Electron-Phonon Coupling at 100 mK

First-pass coupling estimates used in thermalization-time budgeting.

Home Absorber + Source Thermalization Time e-ph Coupling Kapitza Coupling Thermal Conductance

Model

Use the standard normal-metal electron-phonon power flow model:

\[ P_{e-ph}=\Sigma V\left(T_e^5-T_{ph}^5\right), \quad G_{e-ph}=\frac{\partial P}{\partial T_e}\approx 5\Sigma V T^4. \]

At \(T=100\,\mathrm{mK}\):

\[ G_{e-ph}(100\,\mathrm{mK})\approx 5\Sigma V(0.1)^4. \]

Assumptions

ParameterValue used
Ho volume\(V_{Ho}=1\times10^{-16}\,\mathrm{m^3}\)
\(\Sigma\) (effective normal-metal range)\(0.5\times10^9\) to \(3\times10^9\,\mathrm{W\,m^{-3}\,K^{-5}}\)

This first-pass estimate neglects explicit phonon-phonon interface coupling (thermal boundary/Kapitza conductance) at the Ho-membrane interface.

Numerical Results

For \(V_{Ho}=1\times10^{-16}\,\mathrm{m^3}\):

\[ G_{e-ph}(100\,\mathrm{mK})\approx 2.5\times10^{-11}\text{ to }1.5\times10^{-10}\,\mathrm{W/K}. \]

Using current membrane heat capacity \(C_{mem}\approx 0.72\,\mathrm{eV/mK}\approx1.15\times10^{-16}\,\mathrm{J/K}\), the membrane-to-Ho timescale estimate is:

\[ \tau_{mem\leftrightarrow Ho}\approx \frac{C_{mem}}{G_{e-ph}}\approx 0.8\text{ to }4.6\,\mu\mathrm{s}. \]

This is a first-pass scale estimate; interface conductance and local phonon bottlenecks can shift this significantly.
