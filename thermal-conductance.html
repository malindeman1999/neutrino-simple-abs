Choice of \(G\) to meet thermal-timescale priorities.

Design Priority

Adopt pileup-driven design goal from the 50 Hz source case: \(\tau_{th}\approx\bar{\Delta t}/20\), where \(\bar{\Delta t}=20\,\mathrm{ms}\).

\[ \tau_{th,nom}=1.0\,\mathrm{ms}. \]

This page uses the single selected design point \(\bar{\Delta t}/20\).

Heat Capacity Used

At current sizing, \(C_{tot}\) is dominated by Ho:

\[ C_{tot}\approx C_{Ho}+C_{mem}+C_{KID}+C_{cap} \approx 33.6+0.72+7.8\times10^{-4}+8.7\times10^{-4}\;\mathrm{eV/mK} \approx 34.3\;\mathrm{eV/mK}. \]

Convert to SI for \(G\) calculation:

\[ C_{tot}\approx 34.3\times1.602\times10^{-16} \approx 5.49\times10^{-15}\,\mathrm{J/K}. \]

Conductance from \(\tau_{th}=C/G\)

\[ G_{nom}=\frac{C_{tot}}{\tau_{th,nom}} =\frac{5.49\times10^{-15}}{1.0\times10^{-3}} \approx 5.5\times10^{-12}\,\mathrm{W/K}. \]

This is the selected membrane-to-bath conductance target for \(\tau_{mem\rightarrow bath}=\bar{\Delta t}/20\).

4-Leg SiN Geometry at 100 mK

Assume four identical SiN legs at membrane corners, each with:

\[ w_{leg}=5\,\mu\mathrm{m},\quad t_{leg}=t_{mem}=0.5\,\mu\mathrm{m},\quad N=4. \]

Using diffusive conduction:

\[ G = N\,\kappa(T)\,\frac{w_{leg}t_{leg}}{L_{leg}} \quad\Rightarrow\quad L_{leg}=N\,\kappa(T)\,\frac{w_{leg}t_{leg}}{G}. \]

At \(100\,\mathrm{mK}\), use representative low-stress SiN conductivity range \(\kappa_{SiN}\approx(1{-}3)\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm{W/(m\,K)}\) from low-\(T\) literature scaling.

For nominal \(G_{nom}=5.5\times10^{-12}\,\mathrm{W/K}\):

\[ L_{leg}\approx 1.8\text{ to }5.5\,\mathrm{mm} \quad (\kappa_{SiN}=1{-}3\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm{W/(m\,K)}). \]

Using midrange \(\kappa_{SiN}=2\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm{W/(m\,K)}\):

\[ L_{leg}\approx 3.6\,\mathrm{mm}\quad \text{for }G_{nom}. \]

At the selected design point (\(G_{nom}\)), this gives the working leg-length scale.

This indicates 5 \(\mu\mathrm{m}\)-wide straight legs are too conductive for the slow-\(\tau\) goal unless legs are very long/meandered; narrower effective legs or engineered low-\(k\) structures are likely required.

Modeling Note

This conductance estimate neglects electrothermal feedback (ETF). It is a first-pass open-loop thermal estimate for setting membrane-leg targets.
