Internal Thermalization Time

Within-pixel thermalization channels that set pulse-formation speed.

Definition

Use the internal channels (excluding Channel 1) and take the slowest as the effective within-island thermalization time:

\[ \tau_{int} = \max\!\left(\tau_{mem\leftrightarrow Ho},\tau_{K},\tau_{Ho,ph\rightarrow e},\tau_{qp\rightarrow Ho}\right). \]

Design goal: \(\tau_{int}\) should be much faster than the external island-to-bath time constant.

Channel 1: Whole Island to Bath

\[ \tau_{island\rightarrow bath}\approx \frac{C_{tot}}{G_{bath}}. \]

Design choice sets this channel to one-twentieth of the average event spacing at 50 Hz.

\[ \bar{\Delta t}=1/50\,\mathrm{s}=20\,\mathrm{ms},\qquad \tau_{island\rightarrow bath}=\bar{\Delta t}/20=1.0\,\mathrm{ms}. \]

Equivalent single-pole corner frequency (\(f_c\approx 1/(2\pi\tau)\)):

\[ f_{c,island\rightarrow bath}\approx 159\,\mathrm{Hz}. \]

Ratio of Channel-1 \(\tau\) to average spacing:

\[ \frac{\tau_{island\rightarrow bath}}{\bar{\Delta t}}=\frac{1\,\mathrm{ms}}{20\,\mathrm{ms}}=\frac{1}{20}. \]

This is the adopted project target.

Channel 2: Membrane to Ho (Phonon Coupling)

\[ \tau_{mem\leftrightarrow Ho}\approx \frac{C_{mem}}{G_{mem-Ho,ph}}. \]

Using a first-pass \(100\,\mathrm{mK}\) electron-phonon coupling scale for Ho gives \(G_{mem-Ho,ph}\sim 2.5\times10^{-11}\) to \(1.5\times10^{-10}\,\mathrm{W/K}\), which yields:

\[ \tau_{mem\leftrightarrow Ho}\approx 0.8\text{ to }4.6\,\mu\mathrm{s}. \]

See the linked page for the explicit coupling estimate assumptions.

e-ph Coupling

Channel 3: Kapitza Interface Coupling (Ho Phonon to Membrane Phonon)

\[ \tau_{K}\approx \frac{C_{mem}}{G_{K}},\quad G_K=A_{int}h_KT^3. \]

With first-pass interface assumptions at \(100\,\mathrm{mK}\):

\[ G_K\approx 5\times10^{-10}\text{ to }5\times10^{-9}\,\mathrm{W/K}, \quad \tau_K\approx 0.023\text{ to }0.23\,\mu\mathrm{s}. \]

Kapitza Coupling

Channel 4: Ho Phonons to Ho Electrons

\[ \tau_{Ho,ph\rightarrow e}\approx \frac{C_{ph,Ho}}{G_{e-ph,Ho}}. \]

Using first-pass values at \(100\,\mathrm{mK}\): \(C_{ph,Ho}\sim 3.4\times10^{-2}\,\mathrm{eV/mK}\) and \(G_{e-ph,Ho}\sim 2.5\times10^{-11}\) to \(1.5\times10^{-10}\,\mathrm{W/K}\), gives:

\[ \tau_{Ho,ph\rightarrow e}\approx 0.036\text{ to }0.21\,\mu\mathrm{s}. \]

Assumptions come from the same \(100\,\mathrm{mK}\) e-ph coupling model used for Channel 2.

e-ph Coupling

Channel 5: QP in Cap to Ho Thermalization

\[ \tau_{qp\rightarrow Ho}\approx \tau_{diff/escape} + \tau_{trap/relax}. \]

Engineering estimate for current design phase:

\[ \tau_{qp\rightarrow Ho}\approx 0.1\text{ to }2\,\mu\mathrm{s}. \]

Interpretation: quasiparticles/athermal excitations generated in superconducting capping must reach normal-metal Ho and down-convert rapidly. Long \(\tau_{qp\rightarrow Ho}\) broadens pulse rise and creates non-thermal tails.

Overall Criterion

Channel 1 is the external coupling to bath. The controlling internal timescale is the longest of Channels 2-5.

Require:

\[ \tau_{int} \ll \tau_{th} = \frac{C_{tot}}{G_{bath}}. \]

With current design, \(\tau_{th}=1.0\,\mathrm{ms}\) (\(\bar{\Delta t}/20\)), and the first-pass internal estimate is:

\[ \tau_{int}\approx \max(0.8{-}4.6,\;0.023{-}0.23,\;0.036{-}0.21,\;0.1{-}2)\,\mu\mathrm{s} \approx 0.8{-}4.6\,\mu\mathrm{s}. \]

So internal equilibration is much faster than Channel 1, while Channel 1 intentionally sets the pulse-decay timescale at the \(\bar{\Delta t}/20\) design point.

Athermal Long-Lived States

Potential risks include trapped quasiparticles, weakly coupled phonon populations, or interface-localized excitations that retain energy before thermalization.

If present, these produce delayed pulse components and apparent excess noise. The model should include a delayed-energy fraction when fitting pulse shapes.
