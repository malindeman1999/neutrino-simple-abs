163Ho EC Spectrum Clarification

Why atomic structure matters even when calorimetry thermalizes all contained energy.

Core Statement

For each contained event, calorimetry measures all non-neutrino energy:

\[ E_{\mathrm{cal}} \approx Q - E_{\nu}. \]

So there is no extra missing-energy term from electron-capture (EC) atomic physics itself.

Why Atomic Structure Still Shapes the Spectrum

Even with perfect thermalization, the measured spectrum is a distribution over many events, not one fixed energy.

  • EC can occur from different atomic shells (M, N, ...).
  • Each capture channel has different matrix element, vacancy energy, and natural width.
  • These atomic factors modulate the probability density in \(E_{\nu}\), and therefore in \(E_{\mathrm{cal}}\).

Result: line/edge structure and tails in the calorimetric spectrum near the endpoint are intrinsic physics, not detector loss.

Role of X-Rays and Auger Electrons

Dy de-excitation after EC produces characteristic X-rays and Auger/Coster-Kronig electrons.

  • If they remain in the absorber, they thermalize and are counted in \(E_{\mathrm{cal}}\).
  • If a fraction escapes, that becomes a detector response systematic (escape tail), not a new EC energy term.

Practical Interpretation for HOLMES-Style Analyses

  • Calorimeter design controls containment and thermalization.
  • Atomic-structure modeling controls spectral-shape accuracy near the endpoint.
  • Neutrino-mass inference depends on both: detector response and correct EC line-shape physics.
