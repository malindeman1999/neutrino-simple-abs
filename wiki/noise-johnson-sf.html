Johnson \(S_f\): Full vs Simplified

General detuning-capable expression and zero-detuning approximation.

Full (General) Expression

In this project, Johnson phase-noise PSD is computed from the full matrix model \(Y=M^{-1}N\):

\[ S_{\phi,J}(f,\delta f)=\left|[Y_{J,A}(f,\delta f)]_{\phi}\right|^2+\left|[Y_{J,\phi}(f,\delta f)]_{\phi}\right|^2, \]

with uncorrelated amplitude-like and phase-like Johnson source terms.

Then fractional-frequency PSD is

\[ \left(\frac{S_f}{f_0^2}\right)_J(f,\delta f)= \frac{S_{\phi,J}(f,\delta f)}{f_0^2\,\left|d\phi/df\right|_{\delta f}^{\,2}}. \]

Simplified (On-Resonance) Expression

Using \(\left|d\phi/df\right|\approx 4Q_r/f_0\),

\[ \left(\frac{S_f}{f_0^2}\right)_J(f,0)\approx\frac{S_{\phi,J}(f,0)}{(4Q_r)^2}. \]

This is the compact form used for quick checks and scaling arguments.

Expected Convergence Limits

The simplified form is expected to converge to the full model in the limit of:

  • on-resonance operation (\(\delta f\to 0\));
  • small electrothermal-feedback-induced distortion of local phase slope (weak ETF around operating point);
  • small-signal linear response about the bias point.

Outside those limits, use the full matrix-based expression above.

Verification status: convergence behavior has been checked in code: full and simplified forms coincide on resonance and diverge off resonance, as expected.
