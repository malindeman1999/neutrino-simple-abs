Comparison Table

Isotope / Source Material State at 100 mK Decay Type and Endpoint/Q Key Practical Issues Recommendation
Ho-163 (embedded source) Ho is a magnetic normal metal; often used in metallic host (e.g., Au:Ho) for microcalorimetry EC decay, Q ~ 2.86 keV Magnetic/hyperfine heat-capacity contributions at low T; isotope production/purification and controlled implantation/alloying required Recommended for TKID calorimetric concept (low Q, calorimetric endpoint analysis)
Re-187 (source = absorber) Metallic rhenium is superconducting at 100 mK (Tc ~ 1.7 K) beta- decay, endpoint ~ 2.47 keV Very long half-life lowers specific activity; challenging detector/systematics history in practical arrays Possible but generally lower-priority than Ho-163 for new large arrays
Tritium (H-3) Can be molecular solid if condensed at 100 mK, but flagship neutrino experiments use gaseous molecular T2 at higher T beta- decay, endpoint ~ 18.6 keV Higher endpoint reduces near-endpoint statistics for calorimetric approach; containment, permeation, and safety handling are major constraints Best for spectrometer approach (KATRIN-like), not preferred for this TKID calorimetric architecture

Recommendation Summary

For this project, Ho-163 remains the preferred source because its low Q-value is well matched to calorimetric neutrino-mass sensitivity goals and existing cryogenic microcalorimetry development.
