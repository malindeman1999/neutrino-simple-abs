Detector Goal

This design specifies a thermal kinetic inductance detector (TKID) operated as a calorimeter for neutrino mass measurement with low-Q isotopes such as Ho-163.

Thermal regime chain: \(E \rightarrow \text{absorber} \rightarrow \Delta T \rightarrow \text{KID frequency shift}\).

Pixel Architecture

Each pixel contains:

  • Absorber (includes radioactive isotope)
  • Membrane (thermal isolation)
  • KID resonator (thermometer)

Thermal topology: \(\text{Absorber} \leftrightarrow \text{KID} \leftrightarrow \text{Membrane} \leftrightarrow \text{Bath}\)

Core Design Rules

Rule 1: \(\tau_{qp}/\tau_{res}<0.1\). Quasiparticle dynamics should be faster than resonator response so quasiparticle-statistics fluctuations do not significantly add noise to the measured signal.

Rule 2: \(\tau_{res}/\tau_{th}<1\). Resonator response should remain faster than thermal evolution so pulse shape is set by thermal physics, not readout lag, and to maintain margin for stable electrothermal feedback.

Rule 3: phonon phase ASD \(>\) TLS phase ASD (100 Hz). Forces the low-frequency floor to be set by fundamental thermal noise rather than dielectric excess noise.

Rule 5: \(P_0/P_{bif}>0.5\). Soft operating floor to keep readout power high enough for good signal swing and TLS suppression margin.

Rule 6: \(P_{bif,dBm}\) in typical MKID range. Sanity check that the design sits in a realistic readout-power regime for comparable 100 mK, ~1 GHz devices.

Rule 7: \(\Delta T_{event}>1\) mK. Ensures a resolvable thermal step from a Ho event above practical small fluctuations and readout granularity.

Rule 8: \(\Delta T_{event}<100\) mK. Checks that the design is not carrying excess heat capacity for the event energy scale; too much added heat capacity suppresses \(\Delta T\) and reduces signal size.

Rule 9: \(\sigma_{E,th,eV}<\sigma_{E,target}\). Keeps intrinsic thermal fluctuation energy scale below the resolution target budget.

Rule 10: \(T_{bath}>10\) mK (from link estimate). Avoids unrealistically deep inferred bath temperatures from chosen \(P_0/G\), supporting physical consistency.

Rule 11: \(\Delta T_{\mathrm{event}} < \Delta T_{\mathrm{abs-bath}}\). Keeps event heating below electrothermal operating elevation so ETF power margin can pull event heat through the KID channel rather than letting it preferentially leak to the bath.

Rule 12: all eigenvalues of \(M_t\) must have negative real parts. This is the linear stability condition for the coupled electrothermal-resonator dynamics.

Rule 5 applies a soft floor on operating fraction:

Rule 6 checks that \(P_{\mathrm{bif,dBm}}\) lies in the chosen typical range:

Rule 7 requires a resolvable event temperature rise:

\[ \Delta T_{\mathrm{event}} > 1\,\mathrm{mK}. \]

Rule 8 caps event temperature rise to stay in a small-signal regime:

\[ \Delta T_{\mathrm{event}} < 100\,\mathrm{mK}. \]

Rule 9 limits the thermal fluctuation energy scale:

\[ \sigma_{E,\mathrm{th,eV}} < \sigma_{E,\mathrm{target}}, \quad \sigma_{E,\mathrm{target}}=0.1\,\mathrm{eV}\ \text{(default)}. \]

Rule 10 requires inferred bath temperature to stay above 10 mK:

\[ T_{\mathrm{bath}} > 10\,\mathrm{mK}. \]

Rule 11 requires event rise to stay below operating temperature elevation above bath:

\[ \Delta T_{\mathrm{event}} < \Delta T_{\mathrm{abs-bath}}. \]

Rule 12 requires time-domain stability of \(M_t\):

\[ \Re[\lambda_i(M_t)]<0,\ \forall i. \]

Soft goal: choose \(P_0/P_{\mathrm{bif}}\) close to 1 from below.

\[ V_{\mathrm{abs}}=\frac{R}{A_{\mathrm{Ho}}}. \]

Cubic absorber edge and dimensions:

\[ a_{\mathrm{abs}}=V_{\mathrm{abs}}^{1/3},\qquad L_{\mathrm{abs}}=W_{\mathrm{abs}}=t_{\mathrm{abs}}=a_{\mathrm{abs}}. \]

\[ L_{\mathrm{mem}}=L_{\mathrm{abs}}+L_{\mathrm{KID}}+\Delta_{\mathrm{mem}},\qquad W_{\mathrm{mem}}=W_{\mathrm{abs}}+W_{\mathrm{KID}}+\Delta_{\mathrm{mem}}. \]

\[ S_{\mathrm{mem}}=\max(L_{\mathrm{mem}},W_{\mathrm{mem}}). \]
