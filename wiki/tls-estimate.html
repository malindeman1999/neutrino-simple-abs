Model

Use a standard scaling model:

\[ \frac{S_f}{f_0^2}(\nu)=\frac{A\,F\,\tan\delta_{TLS}}{\nu^{\beta}}\left(1+\frac{P_{int}}{P_c}\right)^{-m}. \]

Estimated Scale

Using the above ranges, a practical first-pass fractional-frequency-noise estimate at 100-200 kHz is:

\[ \frac{S_f}{f_0^2}\sim 10^{-19}\text{ to }10^{-17}\ /\mathrm{Hz}. \]

Design target remains keeping this near or below \(\sim10^{-18}/\mathrm{Hz}\) in-band.

Interpretation

  • Lower \(F\) (via larger-gap/larger-area IDC) gives direct improvement.
  • Higher \(P_{int}\) helps through TLS saturation until nonlinearity/heating constraints appear.
  • These are pre-fabrication scaling estimates; final numbers require fitting \(A,\beta,m,P_c\) from your own resonators.
