Count Rate Constraint

Pileup condition: \(P \approx R\,\tau_{resolve}\) with required \(P \ll 1\).

For 50 Hz per pixel operation, design uses \(\tau_{resolve} \approx 5{-}10\,\mu s\), consistent with thermal timing.

Critical Risk Areas

  • TLS noise from capacitor design and dielectric quality.
  • Phonon escape driven by membrane and geometry.
  • Thermal coupling hierarchy violations.
  • Resonator bandwidth not matching pulse times.
  • Fabrication uniformity across very large arrays.

Final Viability Conditions

The architecture is viable if and only if:

  1. Resonator response is faster than the thermal pulse.
  2. TLS noise is below thermal noise.
  3. The thermal system behaves as one node.

Under those conditions, expected performance is:

  • ~100 meV resolution
  • ~50 Hz per pixel
  • Scalable neutrino mass sensitivity
