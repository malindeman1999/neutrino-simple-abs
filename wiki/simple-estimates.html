1) Thermal Frequency Slope from \(\alpha_\phi\)

From the Eq. (4) definition used in the theory page:

\[ \alpha_\phi=-2Q_i\frac{T}{f_r}\frac{df_r}{dT}. \]

At the operating point (\(T\approx T_0\), \(f_r\approx f_0\)):

\[ \frac{df_r}{dT}\approx-\frac{\alpha_\phi f_0}{2Q_iT_0}. \]

2) Temperature Rise per Absorbed Energy

Using island heat capacity \(C\):

\[ \frac{dT}{dE}=\frac{1}{C}. \]

For one absorbed Ho event of energy \(E_{\mathrm{Ho}}\):

\[ \Delta T_{\mathrm{event}}=\frac{E_{\mathrm{Ho}}}{C}. \]

3) Convert Frequency Shift to Phase Shift

Use the small-signal phase slope:

\[ \frac{d\phi}{df}\approx\frac{4Q_r/f_0}{1+(2Q_r\delta f/f_0)^2}, \]

which reduces on resonance (\(\delta f=0\)) to \(d\phi/df\approx 4Q_r/f_0\).

Chain rule:

\[ \frac{d\phi}{dE}=\frac{d\phi}{df}\frac{df_r}{dT}\frac{dT}{dE}. \]

Event-level estimates:

\[ \Delta f_{r,\mathrm{event}}=\frac{df_r}{dT}\Delta T_{\mathrm{event}}, \qquad \Delta\phi_{\mathrm{event}}=\frac{d\phi}{dE}E_{\mathrm{Ho}}. \]

4) Simple Phonon-Noise Estimate in Phase Units

At device temperature \(T_0\), use the low-frequency phonon power ASD:

\[ \sqrt{S_{P,\mathrm{ph}}}(T_0)=\sqrt{4k_BT_0^2G}\quad [\mathrm{W}/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}]. \]

Convert to temperature ASD with thermal conductance:

\[ \sqrt{S_{T,\mathrm{ph}}}(T_0)=\frac{\sqrt{S_{P,\mathrm{ph}}}(T_0)}{G}\quad [\mathrm{K}/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}]. \]

Convert to energy ASD with island heat capacity:

\[ \sqrt{S_{E,\mathrm{ph}}}(T_0)=C\,\sqrt{S_{T,\mathrm{ph}}}(T_0)\quad [\mathrm{J}/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}]. \]

Then apply the same \(d\phi/dE\) chain to estimate low-frequency phase ASD:

\[ \sqrt{S_{\phi,\mathrm{ph}}}^{\,\mathrm{simple}}(T_0)= \left|\frac{d\phi}{dE}\right|\,\sqrt{S_{E,\mathrm{ph}}}(T_0) \quad [\mathrm{rad}/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}]. \]

This is a compact scaling estimate; the full model remains \(Y=M^{-1}N\).

Verification note: for the current nominal settings (\(f_0=1\,\mathrm{GHz}\), \(\delta f=0\), \(f_{\mathrm{demod}}=0\)), this simple phonon phase-ASD estimate matches the full matrix-propagated value \(|[Y_{ph}]_\phi|\) to numerical precision.

5) Thermal Energy-Fluctuation Scale

A simple island thermal-energy fluctuation scale is

\[ \sigma_{E,\mathrm{th}}=\sqrt{k_B T_0^2 C}. \]

In electron-volts:

\[ \sigma_{E,\mathrm{th,eV}}=\frac{\sigma_{E,\mathrm{th}}}{q_e}. \]

Numerical values are reported in Python Estimates under Simple Estimates.
