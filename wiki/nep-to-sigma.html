NEP Integration to Energy Resolution

Computing \(\sigma_E\) from a frequency-dependent NEP spectrum.

Home Theory Noise Sources Signal Processing NEP to Sigma

Definition

Given a one-sided NEP ASD spectrum \(\mathrm{NEP}(f)\) in \(\mathrm{W}/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}\), compute RMS energy resolution by:

\[ \sigma_E = \left(\int_{f_{\min}}^{f_{\max}} \frac{4}{\mathrm{NEP}(f)^2}\,df\right)^{-1/2}. \]

Equivalent FWHM form: \(\Delta E_{\mathrm{FWHM}}=2\sqrt{2\ln2}\,\sigma_E\).

Discrete Integration (Log-Spaced f)

For sampled \((f_i, \mathrm{NEP}_i)\), including log-spaced \(f\), evaluate the integral numerically with trapezoidal integration over \(f\):

\[ \int \frac{4}{\mathrm{NEP}^2}df \approx \sum_i \frac{\frac{4}{\mathrm{NEP}_i^2}+\frac{4}{\mathrm{NEP}_{i+1}^2}}{2}\,(f_{i+1}-f_i), \quad \sigma_E=\left(\int \frac{4}{\mathrm{NEP}^2}df\right)^{-1/2}. \]

Important: do not assume constant \(\Delta f\). Use the actual spacing \(f_{i+1}-f_i\).

Implemented in Sensor.sigma_energy_from_nep_spectrum_J() and Sensor.sigma_energy_from_nep_spectrum_eV().

Scope Note

This \(\sigma_E\) integration is a direct NEP-band integration metric. For full optimal-filter \(\sigma_E\), use the template-weighted expression on Signal Processing.
