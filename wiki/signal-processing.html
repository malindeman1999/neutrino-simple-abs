Pulse Model in I/Q

An event of energy \(E\) produces thermal response

\[ \delta T(\omega)=\frac{E/C}{1+i\omega\tau_{th}}. \]

Readout template in quadrature space:

\[ \mathbf{s}_E(\omega)=\mathbf{g}_T(\omega)\,\delta T(\omega), \quad \mathbf{g}_T(\omega)= \begin{bmatrix} \partial I/\partial T \\ \partial Q/\partial T \end{bmatrix}. \]

Using your impedance coefficients:

\[ \mathbf{s}_E(\omega)= \begin{bmatrix} g_{I,A}\,\alpha_A + g_{I,\phi}\,\alpha_{\phi}\\ g_{Q,A}\,\alpha_A + g_{Q,\phi}\,\alpha_{\phi} \end{bmatrix} \delta T(\omega). \]

Noise and Whitening

From the resonator-bolometer theory page, build source vectors with Eq. (16)-(19), propagate with \(Y=M^{-1}N\), and then map to measured quadratures. The measured noise PSD matrix is

\[ \mathbf{S}_{IQ}(\omega)=\mathbf{G}(\omega)\,\mathbf{S}(\omega)\,\mathbf{G}^{\dagger}(\omega)+\mathbf{S}_{amp}(\omega). \]

Use this same construction for phonon, Johnson, excess electronic/quasiparticle, TLS, and amplifier contributions so filter weights and NEP estimates are internally consistent.

Whitening uses \(\mathbf{W}(\omega)\) such that \(\mathbf{W}\mathbf{S}_{IQ}\mathbf{W}^{\dagger}=\mathbf{I}\), with e.g. Cholesky or eigen decomposition.

Whitened data/template:

\[ \tilde{\mathbf{d}}=\mathbf{W}\mathbf{d}, \qquad \tilde{\mathbf{s}}_1=\mathbf{W}\mathbf{s}_1. \]

Optimal Linear Energy Estimator

For unit-energy template \(\mathbf{s}_1\), the frequency-domain ML/optimal-filter estimator is

\[ \hat{E}=\frac{\int_0^{\infty} \mathbf{s}_1^{\dagger}(\omega)\,\mathbf{S}_{IQ}^{-1}(\omega)\,\mathbf{d}(\omega)\,\frac{d\omega}{2\pi}} {\int_0^{\infty} \mathbf{s}_1^{\dagger}(\omega)\,\mathbf{S}_{IQ}^{-1}(\omega)\,\mathbf{s}_1(\omega)\,\frac{d\omega}{2\pi}}. \]

Estimator variance:

\[ \sigma_E^{-2}=\int_0^{\infty} \mathbf{s}_1^{\dagger}(\omega)\,\mathbf{S}_{IQ}^{-1}(\omega)\,\mathbf{s}_1(\omega) \frac{d\omega}{2\pi}. \]

If you estimate in phase direction directly, use Eq. (20) notation from Theory:

\[ \mathrm{NEP}_{\phi}(\omega)=\left|\frac{[Y_{\mathrm{RMS}}(\omega)]_{\phi}}{(M^{-1})_{\phi,\,P}}\right|. \]

Then convert to an energy-domain noise prediction by weighting \(\mathrm{NEP}_{\phi}(\omega)\) with the same pulse template and integration limits used by the optimal filter.

Practical Processing Steps

  1. Fit or import resonator parameters and sensitivities (\(\alpha,\beta,Q,Q_i,P_0,C,G\)); construct \(M(\omega)\).
  2. Build Eq. (16)-(19) source vectors; propagate with \(Y=M^{-1}N\); assemble modeled \(\mathbf{S}_{IQ}(\omega)\).
  3. Estimate empirical \(\mathbf{S}_{IQ}(\omega)\) from off-event data windows and compare against modeled components.
  4. Build \(\mathbf{s}_1(\omega)\) from calibration pulses.
  5. Apply whitening or directly apply \(\mathbf{S}_{IQ}^{-1}\)-weighted matched filter.
  6. Estimate \(\hat{E}\), then calibrate to physical energy units.
  7. Report both \(\sigma_E\) and phase-direction \(\mathrm{NEP}_{\phi}\); validate residuals and nonlinearity terms linked to \(\beta_A,\beta_\phi\).
