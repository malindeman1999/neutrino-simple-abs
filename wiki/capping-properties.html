Capping Requirement for Electron Capture

For Ho-source de-excitation electrons (few-keV scale), practical superconducting capping guidance is:

  • Minimum capping thickness: \(t_{cap,min}\approx 0.3\,\mu\mathrm{m}\).
  • Preferred design range: \(t_{cap,target}\approx 0.5{-}1.0\,\mu\mathrm{m}\).

The cap should fully overlap the source footprint so no source edge is exposed.

Geometry Used for Heat-Capacity Estimate

Using absorber footprint \(A=10\,\mu\mathrm{m}\times 10\,\mu\mathrm{m}=1\times10^{-10}\,\mathrm{m^2}\):

\[ V_{cap}=A\,t_{cap}. \]

For \(t_{cap}=0.5\,\mu\mathrm{m}\):

\[ V_{cap}=5\times10^{-17}\,\mathrm{m^3}. \]

Cap Heat Capacity at 100 mK

General scaling (material specific):

\[ C_{cap}(100\,\mathrm{mK})=\rho_{cap}V_{cap}c_{cap}(100\,\mathrm{mK}). \]

In detector units:

\[ C_{cap}[\mathrm{eV/mK}] = 6.2415\times10^{15}\, C_{cap}[\mathrm{J/K}]. \]

For \(t_{cap}=0.5\,\mu\mathrm{m}\):

\[ C_{cap}(100\,\mathrm{mK})[\mathrm{eV/mK}] = 6.2415\times10^{15}\,\rho_{cap}(5\times10^{-17})\,c_{cap}(100\,\mathrm{mK}). \]

Numeric phonon estimate (far below \(T_C\)):

\[ c_{ph}(T)=\frac{12\pi^4}{5}\frac{R}{M}\left(\frac{T}{\Theta_D}\right)^3. \]

Using \(\Theta_D\approx 300\,\mathrm{K}\), \(M\approx 240\,\mathrm{g/mol}\) (WSi\(_2\)-like), and \(T=0.1\,\mathrm{K}\):

\[ c_{ph}(100\,\mathrm{mK})\approx 3.0\times10^{-10}\,\mathrm{J/(g\,K)}. \]

With \(\rho_{cap}\approx 9.3\,\mathrm{g/cm^3}\) and \(t_{cap}=0.5\,\mu\mathrm{m}\):

\[ C_{cap}(100\,\mathrm{mK})\approx 1.4\times10^{-19}\,\mathrm{J/K} \approx 8.7\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm{eV/mK}. \]

Thickness range \(0.3{-}1.0\,\mu\mathrm{m}\) scales linearly:

\[ C_{cap}(100\,\mathrm{mK})\approx (5.2\times10^{-4}\ \text{to}\ 1.7\times10^{-3})\,\mathrm{eV/mK}. \]

Use measured film \(c_{cap}(100\,\mathrm{mK})\) and \(\Theta_D\) for your exact WSi process to refine this estimate.

Design Note

Because the cap is superconducting, low-temperature heat capacity is process-dependent (film stoichiometry, thickness, disorder, and magnetic environment). Treat the final \(C_{cap}(100\,\mathrm{mK})\) as a measured input parameter for the final pixel budget.
