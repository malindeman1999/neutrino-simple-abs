Johnson Noise Vectors

Eq. (17)-(18) decomposition into amplitude-like and phase-like source vectors.

Physical Expression

Bolometer resistance Johnson voltage scale:

\[ S_V=4k_B T_0 R_0,\qquad V_e=\sqrt{S_V}=\sqrt{4k_B T_0 R_0}. \]

Units: \(S_V\) in \(\mathrm{V^2/Hz}\), and \(V_e\) in \(\mathrm{V}/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}\).

Johnson voltage appears in both sidebands and is represented by two orthogonal source directions.

Sideband Vector Derivation (Eq. 17)

Define amplitude-like and phase-like sideband vectors:

\[n_{J,A}=\frac{V_e}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\-I_0\end{bmatrix},\qquad n_{J,\phi}=\frac{V_e}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{bmatrix}1\\-1\\0\end{bmatrix}.\]

The third component in \\(n_{J,A}\\) is the correlated Joule-power term from electrical fluctuations.

Sideband Vector Units

Since \(V_e=\sqrt{4k_BT_0R_0}\) is a voltage-noise amplitude, \(V_e\) carries units \(\mathrm{V}/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}\).

Therefore sideband vectors have component-wise units:

\[ \mathrm{units}(n_{J,A})= \begin{bmatrix} \mathrm{V}/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}\\ \mathrm{V}/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}\\ \mathrm{W}/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}} \end{bmatrix}, \qquad \mathrm{units}(n_{J,\phi})= \begin{bmatrix} \mathrm{V}/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}\\ \mathrm{V}/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}\\ 0 \end{bmatrix}. \]

Normalized Vector Derivation (Eq. 18)

Apply demodulation and normalization transforms (Eq. 8, 10, 12) to obtain:

\[N_{J,A}=T_V^{-1}d^{-1}n_{J,A},\qquad N_{J,\phi}=T_V^{-1}d^{-1}n_{J,\phi}.\]

Written explicitly in normalized coordinates (Eq. 18 form):

\[ N_{J,A}= \begin{bmatrix} \sqrt{16k_BT_0/P_0}\\ 0\\ -\sqrt{4k_BT_0P_0} \end{bmatrix}, \qquad N_{J,\phi}= \begin{bmatrix} 0\\ i\sqrt{16k_BT_0/P_0}\\ 0 \end{bmatrix}. \]

These are the vectors used in \\(Y=M^{-1}N\\) for noise propagation.

Vector Component Units

The vectors are defined in the \((r,\phi,T)\) state basis and have per-component units:

\[ \mathrm{units}(N_{J,A})= \begin{bmatrix} 1/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}\\ 1/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}\\ \mathrm{W}/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}} \end{bmatrix}, \qquad \mathrm{units}(N_{J,\phi})= \begin{bmatrix} 1/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}\\ 1/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}\\ 0 \end{bmatrix}. \]

Role In NEP and Electronic-Noise Scaling

Propagated Johnson outputs are \(Y_{J,A}=M^{-1}N_{J,A}\) and \(Y_{J,\phi}=M^{-1}N_{J,\phi}\).

The amplitude-like and phase-like Johnson source terms are treated as uncorrelated. After propagation by \(M^{-1}\), both can contribute to the demodulated phase output, so the total Johnson phase-noise PSD is the quadrature sum of the two propagated phase components:

\[ S_{\phi,J}(f)=\left|[Y_{J,A}(f)]_{\phi}\right|^2+\left|[Y_{J,\phi}(f)]_{\phi}\right|^2. \]

General model (used in this project): compute Johnson-driven phase noise from the full matrix model \(Y=M^{-1}N\), including detuning \(\delta f\), then convert to fractional-frequency noise with the local phase slope:

\[ \left(\frac{S_f}{f_0^2}\right)_J(f,\delta f)= \frac{S_{\phi,J}(f,\delta f)}{f_0^2\,\left|d\phi/df\right|_{\delta f}^{\,2}}. \]

This keeps the treatment general in detuning via both \(S_{\phi,J}\) and \((d\phi/df)_{\delta f}\).

Zero-detuning consistency check: at \(\delta f\!\to\!0\), \(\left|d\phi/df\right|\approx 4Q_r/f_0\), so the expression collapses to

\[ \left(\frac{S_f}{f_0^2}\right)_J(f,0)=\frac{S_{\phi,J}(f,0)}{(4Q_r)^2}. \]

Phase-direction NEP uses Eq. (20):

\[ \mathrm{NEP}_{\phi,J}(\omega)=\left|\frac{[Y_{J,\mathrm{RMS}}(\omega)]_{\phi}}{(M^{-1})_{\phi,\,P}}\right|. \]

Excess electronic/quasiparticle-generation noise is treated as scaled Johnson vectors (see Electronic Noise Vector).
