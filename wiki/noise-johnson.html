Johnson Noise Vectors

Eq. (17)-(18) decomposition into amplitude-like and phase-like source vectors.

HomeTheoryNoise SourcesJohnsonSignal Processing

Physical Expression

Bolometer resistance Johnson voltage scale:

\[V_e=\sqrt{4k_B T_0 R_0}.\]

Johnson voltage appears in both sidebands and is represented by two orthogonal source directions.

Nominal Frequency Setting

For calculations in this wiki, use nominal detector frequency \(f_0=1.0\,\mathrm{GHz}\) when converting phase noise to fractional-frequency noise.

Equivalent PSD Form

The same source can be written in standard Nyquist form:

\[ S_V=4k_BT_0R_0,\qquad S_I=\frac{4k_BT_0}{R_0}. \]

The vector formalism below is preferred for this wiki because it preserves the electrical and correlated thermal components needed by \(Y=M^{-1}N\).

Sideband Vector Derivation (Eq. 17)

Define amplitude-like and phase-like sideband vectors:

\[n_{J,A}=\frac{V_e}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\-I_0\end{bmatrix},\qquad n_{J,\phi}=\frac{V_e}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{bmatrix}1\\-1\\0\end{bmatrix}.\]

The third component in \\(n_{J,A}\\) is the correlated Joule-power term from electrical fluctuations.

Normalized Vector Derivation (Eq. 18)

Apply demodulation and normalization transforms (Eq. 8, 10, 12) to obtain:

\[N_{J,A}=T_V^{-1}d^{-1}n_{J,A},\qquad N_{J,\phi}=T_V^{-1}d^{-1}n_{J,\phi}.\]

Written explicitly in normalized coordinates (Eq. 18 form):

\[ N_{J,A}= \begin{bmatrix} \sqrt{16k_BT_0/P_0}\\ 0\\ -\sqrt{4k_BT_0P_0} \end{bmatrix}, \qquad N_{J,\phi}= \begin{bmatrix} 0\\ i\sqrt{16k_BT_0/P_0}\\ 0 \end{bmatrix}. \]

These are the vectors used in \\(Y=M^{-1}N\\) for noise propagation.

Role In NEP and Electronic-Noise Scaling

Propagated Johnson outputs are \(Y_{J,A}=M^{-1}N_{J,A}\) and \(Y_{J,\phi}=M^{-1}N_{J,\phi}\), combined in quadrature for total Johnson output noise.

Fractional-frequency Johnson noise used for TLS scaling can be written from phase noise as:

\[ \left(\frac{S_f}{f_0^2}\right)_J(f)=\frac{S_{\phi,J}(f)}{(4Q_r)^2}, \]

using small-detuning phase slope \(d\phi/df\approx 4Q_r/f_0\).

Phase-direction NEP uses Eq. (20):

\[ \mathrm{NEP}_{\phi,J}(\omega)=\left|\frac{[Y_{J,\mathrm{RMS}}(\omega)]_{\phi}}{(M^{-1})_{\phi,\,P}}\right|. \]

Excess electronic/quasiparticle-generation noise is treated as scaled Johnson vectors (see Electronic Noise Vector).
