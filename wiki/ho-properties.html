Material and Magnetic State

For neutrino-mass operation, assume the radioactive source isotope is \(^{163}\mathrm{Ho}\).

At \(100\,\mathrm{mK}\), Ho is a normal (non-superconducting) magnetic material; low-temperature magnetic and hyperfine effects can strongly affect heat capacity.

Design implication: source-related magnetic contributions should be treated as first-order terms in the pixel heat-capacity budget.

Count Rate from Ho Volume

For source volume \(V_{\mathrm{Ho}}\), activity is

\[ R_{\mathrm{pixel}}=\lambda N,\quad \lambda=\frac{\ln 2}{T_{1/2}},\quad N=\frac{\rho_{\mathrm{Ho}}V_{\mathrm{Ho}}}{M_{\mathrm{Ho}}}N_A f_{163}. \]

Using \(T_{1/2}(^{163}\mathrm{Ho})\approx 4570\,\mathrm{y}\), \(\rho_{\mathrm{Ho}}\approx 8.8\,\mathrm{g/cm^3}\), \(M_{\mathrm{Ho}}\approx163\,\mathrm{g/mol}\):

\[ R_{\mathrm{pixel}}\approx 1.56\times10^{17}\,V_{\mathrm{Ho}}[\mathrm{m^3}]\,f_{163}\;\mathrm{Hz}. \]

Heat Capacity from Ho Volume

Volume scaling:

\[ C_{\mathrm{Ho}}(T)=\rho_{\mathrm{Ho}}V_{\mathrm{Ho}}\,c_{\mathrm{Ho}}(T). \]

For \(V_{\mathrm{Ho}}=1\times10^{-16}\,\mathrm{m^3}\):

\[ m_{\mathrm{Ho}}\approx 8.8\times10^{-10}\,\mathrm{g}, \quad C_{\mathrm{Ho}}(T)\approx 8.8\times10^{-10}\,c_{\mathrm{Ho}}(T)\;\mathrm{J/K}. \]

Unit conversion:

\[ 1\,\mathrm{J/K}=6.2415\times10^{15}\,\mathrm{eV/mK}, \quad C_{\mathrm{Ho}}[\mathrm{eV/mK}]=6.2415\times10^{15}C_{\mathrm{Ho}}[\mathrm{J/K}]. \]

So for \(V_{\mathrm{Ho}}=1\times10^{-16}\,\mathrm{m^3}\):

\[ C_{\mathrm{Ho}}(T)[\mathrm{eV/mK}]\approx 5.49\times10^6\;c_{\mathrm{Ho}}(T)[\mathrm{J/(g\,K)}]. \]

Low-Temperature Example at 100 mK

Using the Ho-metal low-\(T\) fit form reported in the literature,

\[ C_p = 2.26T^3 + 10T + C_N\quad (\mathrm{mJ/mol\,K}), \]

and inserting only the explicit non-nuclear terms at \(T=0.1\,\mathrm{K}\):

\[ C_{p,\mathrm{Ho}}(100\,\mathrm{mK})\approx 1.00\,\mathrm{mJ/(mol\,K)} \Rightarrow c_{\mathrm{Ho}}(100\,\mathrm{mK})\approx 6.13\times10^{-6}\,\mathrm{J/(g\,K)}. \]

For \(V_{\mathrm{Ho}}=1\times10^{-16}\,\mathrm{m^3}\):

\[ C_{\mathrm{Ho}}(100\,\mathrm{mK})\approx 5.39\times10^{-15}\,\mathrm{J/K} \approx 33.6\,\mathrm{eV/mK}. \]

Important: this numeric example omits \(C_N\); full empirical values near 100 mK including magnetic/hyperfine terms can be larger.
