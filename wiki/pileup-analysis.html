Pileup Probability vs Time Constant

Estimate at 50 Hz average source rate using a strict pulse-overlap criterion.

Home Performance & Risks Pileup Analysis Energy Window Criterion

Assumptions

  • Average event rate: \(R=50\,\mathrm{Hz}\)
  • Average spacing: \(\bar{\Delta t}=1/R=20\,\mathrm{ms}=20000\,\mu s\)
  • Single-pulse shape tail modeled as exponential \(\propto e^{-t/\tau}\)
  • Pileup criterion: pileup occurs if more than \(1/20000\) of first-pulse area remains when second pulse begins

Tail-area fraction after delay \(\Delta t\):

\[ f_{tail}(\Delta t)=e^{-\Delta t/\tau}. \]

Pileup threshold delay:

\[ e^{-\Delta t/\tau}>1/20000\Rightarrow \Delta t<\tau\ln(20000)\approx 9.90\,\tau. \]

Derivation page: Energy Window Criterion

For Poisson arrivals, per-event pileup probability:

\[ P_{pileup}=1-e^{-R\,(9.90\tau)}. \]

Recommendation

Selected design goal for this project: \(\tau=\bar{\Delta t}/20\) (1.0 ms).

This corresponds to the table row with overlap window \(9.90\tau=9.90\,\mathrm{ms}\) and raw Poisson overlap probability \(\sim39\%\) under the strict worst-case criterion.

This choice is intentionally conservative with respect to integration-window bias control and assumes model-based pulse fitting will further reduce effective energy bias from overlaps.

Important: this is conservative. In practice, pulse fitting/deconvolution can recover substantially better energy estimates than worst-case direct integration, reducing effective bias from overlaps.
