Excess Electronic Noise Vector

Eq. (19) quasiparticle-generation noise as scaled Johnson vectors.

HomeTheoryNoise SourcesElectronicSignal Processing

Physical Expression

The paper models excess electronic noise from microwave-driven quasiparticle generation as:

\[P_D=\sqrt{4E_{qp}P_0},\qquad M_e\approx\sqrt{\frac{E_{qp}}{k_B T_0}}.\]

Noise Vector Derivation (Eq. 19)

Assumption: this source has the same component structure as Johnson noise (electrical sidebands plus correlated thermal-power component), but larger magnitude.

Therefore:

\[N_{g,A}=M_eN_{J,A},\qquad N_{g,\phi}=M_eN_{J,\phi}.\]

Explicitly:

\[ N_{g,A}= \begin{bmatrix} M_e\sqrt{16k_BT_0/P_0}\\ 0\\ -M_e\sqrt{4k_BT_0P_0} \end{bmatrix}, \qquad N_{g,\phi}= \begin{bmatrix} 0\\ iM_e\sqrt{16k_BT_0/P_0}\\ 0 \end{bmatrix}. \]

Total excess electronic contribution is obtained by quadrature sum of propagated outputs from these two vectors.

Vector Component Units

As with Johnson vectors, \(N_g\) vectors live in the mixed-unit \((r,\phi,T)\) basis:

\[ \mathrm{units}(N_{g,A})= \begin{bmatrix} 1/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}\\ 1/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}\\ \mathrm{W}/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}} \end{bmatrix}, \qquad \mathrm{units}(N_{g,\phi})= \begin{bmatrix} 1/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}\\ 1/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}\\ 0 \end{bmatrix}. \]

Because \(N_{g,A}=M_eN_{J,A}\) and \(N_{g,\phi}=M_eN_{J,\phi}\), units are identical to the Johnson \(N\)-vectors.

Propagation and Comparison

\[Y_{g,A}=M^{-1}N_{g,A},\qquad Y_{g,\phi}=M^{-1}N_{g,\phi}.\]

Use these to compute \\(\mathrm{NEP}_{\phi,g}\\) and compare against phonon contribution to verify phonon-dominant operation.
