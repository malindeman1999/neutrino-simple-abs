Paper Basis and Scope

This page now follows the resonator-bolometer model in M. A. Lindeman, J. Appl. Phys. 116, 024506 (2014), with emphasis on Eq. (4) and Eq. (10)-(20).

Model objective for this project: compute the measured quadrature noise, convert it to phase-direction noise, and then convert that to calorimeter NEP using the bolometer responsivity.

Nominal calculation setting for this wiki: \(f_0=1.0\,\mathrm{GHz}\) unless a section explicitly overrides it.

State and Measurement Vectors

Define a small-signal state vector around the operating point:

\[ \mathbf{x}(\omega) = \begin{bmatrix} \delta T(\omega) \\ \delta x(\omega) \\ \delta y(\omega) \end{bmatrix} \quad x \equiv \frac{f-f_0}{f_0},\; y \equiv \frac{1}{2Q_i} \]

Measured baseband quadratures are:

\[ \mathbf{m}(\omega)= \begin{bmatrix} \delta I(\omega) \\ \delta Q(\omega) \end{bmatrix} \]

Linearized Forward Model

Near resonance, the readout is linearized as:

\[ \mathbf{m}(\omega)=\mathbf{R}(\omega)\,\mathbf{x}(\omega)+\mathbf{n}_{amp}(\omega) \]

with Jacobian

\[ \mathbf{R}(\omega)= \begin{bmatrix} \partial I/\partial T & \partial I/\partial x & \partial I/\partial y \\ \partial Q/\partial T & \partial Q/\partial x & \partial Q/\partial y \end{bmatrix} \]

The thermal-to-resonator chain can be factored as:

\[ \mathbf{x}(\omega)=\mathbf{H}(\omega)\,\mathbf{u}(\omega) \]

Use the source vector

\[ \mathbf{u}(\omega)= \begin{bmatrix} p_E(\omega)\\ p_{TFN}(\omega)\\ n_{TLS}(\omega)\\ n_{GR}(\omega)\\ \delta T_b(\omega) \end{bmatrix} \]

with explicit transfer matrix

\[ \mathbf{H}(\omega)= \begin{bmatrix} \dfrac{1}{C(1+i\omega\tau_{th})} & \dfrac{1}{C(1+i\omega\tau_{th})} & 0 & 0 & \dfrac{G/C}{1+i\omega\tau_{th}}\\[1.1em] \dfrac{k_T}{1+i\omega\tau_{res}} & \dfrac{k_T}{1+i\omega\tau_{res}} & k_{x,TLS} & k_{x,GR} & \dfrac{k_T\,G/C}{1+i\omega\tau_{res}}\\[1.1em] \dfrac{\ell_T}{1+i\omega\tau_{res}} & \dfrac{\ell_T}{1+i\omega\tau_{res}} & k_{y,TLS} & k_{y,GR} & \dfrac{\ell_T\,G/C}{1+i\omega\tau_{res}} \end{bmatrix} \]

This writes thermal and resonator poles explicitly while allowing direct TLS/GR injection into frequency-like and dissipation-like channels.

Eq. (4): Sensitivities \( \alpha \) and \( \beta \)

Let the KID complex impedance be \(Z(T,I)=Z'(T,I)+iZ''(T,I)\). Eq. (4) defines thermal and current sensitivities split into amplitude-like and phase-like channels.

Thermal sensitivities from \(dZ/dT\):

\[ \alpha_{\phi}\equiv \frac{1}{2}\,\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial T}, \qquad \alpha_{A}\equiv \frac{1}{2}\,\frac{\partial \ln A}{\partial T}, \]

with \(\phi=\arg Z\) and \(A=|Z|\). Equivalently, using \(Z\):

\[ \frac{\partial \ln A}{\partial T}=\Re\!\left(\frac{1}{Z}\frac{\partial Z}{\partial T}\right), \qquad \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial T}=\Im\!\left(\frac{1}{Z}\frac{\partial Z}{\partial T}\right). \]

Current sensitivities from \(dZ/dI\):

\[ \beta_{\phi}\equiv \frac{1}{2}\,\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial I}, \qquad \beta_{A}\equiv \frac{1}{2}\,\frac{\partial \ln A}{\partial I}, \]

equivalently

\[ \frac{\partial \ln A}{\partial I}=\Re\!\left(\frac{1}{Z}\frac{\partial Z}{\partial I}\right), \qquad \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial I}=\Im\!\left(\frac{1}{Z}\frac{\partial Z}{\partial I}\right). \]

Eq. (10)-(13): Normalized Sideband-to-Demodulated Model

Eq. (10) defines voltage normalization (\(V_0=I_0R_0/2\)). Eq. (11) is the central linear system \(M\,Y=N\), where \(Y=[r,\phi,T]^T\) are demodulated normalized coordinates and \(N\) is the source/noise vector.

Eq. (12) gives the coordinate transforms from sideband variables to demodulated normalized variables. Eq. (13) expands \(M(\omega)\) explicitly in terms of \(Q, Q_i, \alpha, \beta, P_0, C, G, T_0\), and detuning.

Interpretation for implementation: build \(M(\omega)\) directly from measured or fitted resonator parameters, then solve \(Y=M^{-1}N\) for each source and frequency bin.

Eq. (14)-(15): Time-Domain Dynamics and Pulse Model

Eq. (14) converts the frequency-domain model to time-domain dynamics \(\dot{Y}=M_tY+D_1^{-1}N\), where \(M_t\) is obtained from \(M(\omega)\).

Eq. (15) gives explicit coupled ODEs for \(r(t)\), \(\phi(t)\), and \(T(t)\) on resonance. The absorbed calorimeter power enters as an additive source term \(P(t)/C\) in the thermal equation.

Use this form to check stability (all eigenvalues of \(M_t\) must have negative real parts), pulse shape, and thermal/electrical coupling behavior before noise propagation.

Eq. (16)-(20): Noise Vectors and NEP

Eq. (16): phonon noise enters as a thermal-band power source with RMS scale \(\sqrt{4k_BT_b^2G}\).

Eq. (17): Johnson noise is represented in sideband form with separate amplitude-like and phase-like source vectors.

Eq. (18): transformed normalized Johnson vectors \(N_{J,A}\) and \(N_{J,\phi}\), including the correlated Joule-power fluctuation component in the thermal entry.

Eq. (19): excess electronic (quasiparticle generation) noise is modeled as a multiplier of the Johnson vectors, \(N_g=M_eN_J\), with \(M_e\sim\sqrt{E_{qp}/k_BT_0}\).

Eq. (20): phase-direction NEP is computed from output phase noise divided by phase responsivity to power:

\[ \mathrm{NEP}_{\phi}(\omega)=\left|\frac{[Y_{\mathrm{RMS}}(\omega)]_{\phi}}{(M^{-1})_{\phi,\,P}}\right|. \]

This is the core equation to convert modeled/measured phase noise into calorimeter-equivalent input power noise.

Noise PSD Matrix \( \mathbf{S} \) and Mapping to I/Q

Combine the blocks:

\[ \mathbf{m}(\omega)=\underbrace{\mathbf{R}(\omega)\mathbf{H}(\omega)}_{\mathbf{G}(\omega)}\,\mathbf{u}(\omega)+\mathbf{n}_{amp}(\omega) \]

so \(\mathbf{G}(\omega)\) is the matrix that maps each physical noise source into \(I\) and \(Q\).

Define your source cross-spectral density matrix as

\[ \mathbf{S}(\omega)\equiv\mathbf{S}_u(\omega)= \begin{bmatrix} S_{EE} & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & S_{TFN} & 0 & 0 & S_{TFN,b}\\ 0 & 0 & S_{TLS} & S_{TLS,GR} & 0\\ 0 & 0 & S_{GR,TLS} & S_{GR} & 0\\ 0 & S_{b,TFN} & 0 & 0 & S_{bb} \end{bmatrix} \]

(set off-diagonal terms to zero if you assume independent sources).

A common explicit TLS term is

\[ S_{TLS}(f)=A_{TLS}\,f^{-\beta}, \]

which is inserted in the \((3,3)\) element of \(\mathbf{S}(\omega)\) (and optionally correlated terms).

Then with amplifier/readout noise \(\mathbf{S}_{amp}(\omega)\):

\[ \mathbf{S}_{IQ}(\omega)=\mathbf{G}(\omega)\,\mathbf{S}(\omega)\,\mathbf{G}^{\dagger}(\omega)+\mathbf{S}_{amp}(\omega) \]

Here \(\mathbf{S}_{IQ}\) has entries \(S_{II},S_{QQ},S_{IQ}\), including cross-correlation.

Calorimeter Modeling Workflow

  • Fit \(S_{21}(f,T,P)\) to extract \(Q_i, f_r\), and derivatives needed for Eq. (4) sensitivities.
  • Construct \(M(\omega)\) with Eq. (10)-(13) and verify stable \(M_t\) from Eq. (14)-(15).
  • Build source vectors for phonon, Johnson, excess electronic, TLS, and readout noise using Eq. (16)-(19).
  • Propagate each source via \(Y=M^{-1}N\); sum output noise in quadrature (with correlations where needed).
  • Compute \(\mathrm{NEP}_{\phi}\) with Eq. (20), then feed the resulting PSD into the optimal filter on the Signal Processing page.

Notation convention used here: \(T_b\) is bath temperature and \(T_0\) is bolometer operating-point temperature under bias.
