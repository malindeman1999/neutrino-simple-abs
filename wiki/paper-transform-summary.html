Resonator-Bolometer Transform Summary

Concise map of the coordinate and normalization transforms used in the 2014 paper.

Home Theory M Matrix Transform Summary Noise Sources

Purpose

The paper uses a transformation chain to convert sideband voltages into normalized demodulated coordinates, then solves the coupled linear system in that basis.

Main references in the paper: Eq. (10)-(13), with \(M(\omega)Y(\omega)=N(\omega)\) in Eq. (11).

Transformation Chain

\[ \text{USB/LSB sideband variables} \xrightarrow{\text{demod / coordinate transform}} \text{demodulated quadratures} \xrightarrow{\text{normalization}} Y=[r,\phi,T]^T. \]

In compact notation used across this wiki:

\[ Y = T_V^{-1}\,d^{-1}\,y, \]

where \(y\) is the sideband-domain vector, \(T_V\) is the sideband-to-demodulated transform, and \(d\) applies the paper's normalization scale factors.

Where the Matrix Model Enters

After transformation/normalization, the dynamics and source coupling are solved in normalized coordinates:

\[ M(\omega)\,Y(\omega)=N(\omega),\qquad Y(\omega)=M^{-1}(\omega)\,N(\omega). \]

\(M(\omega)\) coefficients are given by Eq. (13). See M Matrix.

Units and Interpretation

Raw demodulated \(I/Q\) are not automatically in physical phase radians.

The paper's normalization puts variables into a model-consistent basis; converting a measured channel to absolute radians or absolute amplitude requires calibration at a chosen reference plane.

So: transform + normalization defines the coordinate system; experimental calibration defines physical scaling in those coordinates.

Readout Noise Placement

If first-stage amplifier noise is injected after the resonator output, handle it at the output plane:

  • Represent readout noise in USB/LSB (or equivalent I/Q) coordinates.
  • Apply the same transform to map to amplitude/phase coordinates.
  • Add it to modeled output noise covariance/PSD at the same reference plane.

Avoid propagating this post-resonator readout term through \(M^{-1}\), which is for internal device/source vectors.

USB/LSB Readout-Noise Mapping

Given a readout-noise vector defined in sideband coordinates with no thermal component,

\[ y_{\mathrm{ro}}= \begin{bmatrix} v_{\mathrm{USB}}\\ v_{\mathrm{LSB}}^*\\ 0 \end{bmatrix}, \]

use the same paper transform to map it into normalized coordinates:

\[ N_{\mathrm{ro}}=T_V^{-1}d^{-1}y_{\mathrm{ro}}. \]

This places readout noise in the same normalized coordinate basis as \(Y\) and \(N\) in Eq. (11).

For post-resonator amplifier noise, use this mapping for coordinate conversion, then add the resulting contribution at the output noise/covariance level at a common reference plane.

50 Ohm Matched Amplifier Mapping

For a matched source/load with \(R=50\,\Omega\), amplifier noise temperature \(T_n\) maps to input-referred voltage-noise PSD as:

\[ S_V = 4k_B T_n R \quad [\mathrm{V}^2/\mathrm{Hz}], \qquad v_n=\sqrt{4k_B T_n R}\quad [\mathrm{V}/\sqrt{\mathrm{Hz}}]. \]

For an isotropic sideband-noise model, assign equal USB/LSB variances and zero cross-correlation unless measured otherwise:

\[ \langle |v_{\mathrm{USB}}|^2\rangle=\langle |v_{\mathrm{LSB}}|^2\rangle, \qquad \langle v_{\mathrm{USB}}v_{\mathrm{LSB}}\rangle=0. \]

Then map \(y_{\mathrm{ro}}\) to normalized coordinates with \(N_{\mathrm{ro}}=T_V^{-1}d^{-1}y_{\mathrm{ro}}\).

Caveat: keep one explicit reference plane (resonator output, cable output, amp input, etc.). Include all gain/loss/attenuation when referring noise from one plane to another to avoid incorrect scaling or double counting.

Practical Interpretation for This Model

In this model, USB/LSB are the readout sideband voltage variables at the resonator/feedline readout plane used by the paper's small-signal formalism.

They are not automatically raw ADC volts unless the full RF/IF chain is explicitly mapped to that same plane and scaling.

Assumption used here: the feedline is well matched to the first-stage preamplifier input, so reflection/mismatch effects are neglected in the USB/LSB-to-normalized mapping.

If feedline loss is negligible and the resonator output is connected directly to the first-stage preamplifier input, then the resonator/feedline plane and amp-input plane are effectively the same for this mapping.

In that direct-coupled case, the transform steps above are sufficient: form USB/LSB readout noise at that plane, apply \(N_{\mathrm{ro}}=T_V^{-1}d^{-1}y_{\mathrm{ro}}\), and add the contribution at output-noise/covariance level.
