Radioactive Source Geometry and Coverage

The radioactive source must be fully covered by the absorber so all emitted electrons are captured in the absorber volume.

Coverage condition:

\[ A_{source} \le A_{absorber}, \qquad A_{absorber}=10\,\mu\mathrm{m}\times10\,\mu\mathrm{m}. \]

This sets the source footprint target to be within the full absorber top area, with no exposed source edge.

Define source volume as \(V_{\mathrm{Ho}}=A_{\mathrm{source}}t_{\mathrm{Ho}}\), with \(A_{\mathrm{source}}\le A_{\mathrm{absorber}}\).

Superconducting Capping

To improve containment of Ho de-excitation electrons, use superconducting capping over the full source footprint.

  • Minimum cap thickness: \(t_{cap,min}\approx 0.3\,\mu\mathrm{m}\)
  • Target cap thickness: \(t_{cap,target}\approx 0.5{-}1.0\,\mu\mathrm{m}\)

The cap must fully overlap the source laterally so no source edge is exposed.

Heat-capacity summary at \(100\,\mathrm{mK}\):

\[ C_{cap}(100\,\mathrm{mK})\approx 8.7\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm{eV/mK}\quad (t_{cap}=0.5\,\mu\mathrm{m}) \]

\[ C_{cap}(100\,\mathrm{mK})\approx 5.2\times10^{-4}\text{ to }1.7\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm{eV/mK}\quad (t_{cap}=0.3{-}1.0\,\mu\mathrm{m}) \]

At this level, the superconducting capping heat capacity is negligible compared with the Ho-source contribution.

Membrane Area and Heat Capacity Summary

Membrane footprint must allocate room for source+capping, KID, spacing, and wiring.

Example area allocation summary:

\[ A_{mem,target}\approx 3750\,\mu\mathrm{m}^2\;(\sim61\times61\,\mu\mathrm{m}). \]

Heat-capacity summary at \(100\,\mathrm{mK}\):

\[ C_{mem}(100\,\mathrm{mK})\approx 0.72\,\mathrm{eV/mK} \]

Typical island range (50-100 \(\mu\mathrm{m}\) square):

\[ C_{mem}(100\,\mathrm{mK})\approx 0.48\text{ to }1.92\,\mathrm{eV/mK}. \]

At this design point, membrane heat capacity is negligible compared with Ho-source heat capacity.

for example \(A_{KID}=30\times30\,\mu\mathrm{m}^2\) and \(t_{KID}=50\,\mathrm{nm}\).

\[ C_{KID}(100\,\mathrm{mK})\approx 4.7\times10^{-4}\text{ to }1.6\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm{eV/mK}. \]

At this level, KID heat capacity is negligible compared with Ho-source heat capacity.

Heat-Capacity Priority

For this current sizing, \(C_{\mathrm{Ho}}\) dominates the budget.

Design significance threshold: only effects larger than \(\sim10\%\) of total heat capacity are treated as first-order.

Under that criterion, both membrane and KID heat capacities are negligible (\(<10\%\) effects).

Using current heat-capacity sizing and \(\tau_{th,nom}=1.0\,\mathrm{ms}\):

Within-Absorber Thermalization Time

Internal thermalization is set by the slowest of three channels:

\[ \tau_{int}=\max\!\left(\tau_{mem\rightarrow bath},\tau_{mem\leftrightarrow Ho},\tau_{K},\tau_{Ho,ph\rightarrow e},\tau_{qp\rightarrow Ho}\right). \]

Where:

  • \(\tau_{mem\rightarrow bath}\sim C_{mem}/G_{mem}\)
  • \(\tau_{mem\leftrightarrow Ho}\sim C_{mem}/G_{ph,mem-Ho}\)
  • \(\tau_{Ho,ph\rightarrow e}\sim C_{ph,Ho}/G_{e-ph,Ho}\)
  • \(\tau_{qp\rightarrow Ho}\): qp/athermal energy transfer from superconducting cap into normal-metal Ho

Design requirement: this internal thermalization time should be much faster than the main membrane C/G pulse time.

Also check for long-lived athermal states (trapped qp / weakly coupled phonons) that can delay thermalization and add slow pulse tails.
