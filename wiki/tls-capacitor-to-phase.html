TLS: Capacitor Noise to Phase Noise

Standard resonator-literature mapping used outside the Lindeman vector formalism.

Home Theory Noise Sources TLS TLS Mapping

1) Dielectric/TLS Fluctuations in the Capacitor

TLS in dielectric regions cause fluctuations in effective permittivity and capacitance:

\[ \delta \epsilon(t)\rightarrow \delta C(t). \]

For an LC resonator, first-order frequency shift is

\[ \frac{\delta f_r}{f_r}\approx -\frac{1}{2}\frac{\delta C}{C_{\mathrm{eff}}}, \]

with participation/filling-factor weighting for where TLS-hosting dielectrics store electric energy.

2) Frequency-Noise PSD Model

Define fractional-frequency-noise PSD

\[ S_y(\nu)\equiv S_{\delta f_r/f_r}(\nu)=\frac{S_f(\nu)}{f_r^2}. \]

Gao-style semi-empirical modeling fits

\[ S_y(\nu)\propto \nu^{-\beta} \left(1+\frac{P_{\mathrm{int}}}{P_c}\right)^{-m}, \]

and in practical MKID parameterizations includes participation/loss terms such as \(F\tan\delta_{\mathrm{TLS}}\).

3) Map to Demodulated Phase Noise

Phase responds to resonance-frequency motion through local slope:

\[ \delta\phi\approx \left(\frac{d\phi}{df}\right)\delta f_r \quad\Rightarrow\quad S_\phi(\nu)\approx \left|\frac{d\phi}{df}\right|^2 S_f(\nu). \]

Equivalent fractional form:

\[ S_y(\nu)=\frac{S_\phi(\nu)}{f_r^2\left|d\phi/df\right|^2}. \]

On resonance, small-signal slope gives \(\left|d\phi/df\right|\approx 4Q_r/f_r\), so

\[ S_\phi(\nu)\approx (4Q_r)^2 S_y(\nu). \]

4) Sideband Interpretation

In I/Q language, phase modulation corresponds to antisymmetric USB/LSB sidebands (opposite sign), while amplitude modulation is symmetric. This is why TLS frequency fluctuations appear primarily in the phase quadrature.

5) Practical Mapping: \(C\)-Fluctuations \(\rightarrow\) TLS Noise \(\rightarrow\) USB Voltage

This section gives the direct algebra used to convert a TLS fractional-frequency-noise model into equivalent capacitor sideband voltage noise.

Step A: frequency shift from capacitance shift

\[ \frac{\delta f}{f_0}\approx -\frac{1}{2}\frac{\delta C}{C_0}. \]

Step B: PSD mapping

Define \(S_y(\delta\omega)\equiv S_f(\delta\omega)/f_0^2\). Then

\[ S_{\delta C/C_0}(\delta\omega)=4\,S_y(\delta\omega),\qquad S_{\delta C}(\delta\omega)=4C_0^2\,S_y(\delta\omega). \]

Equivalent capacitance ASD:

\[ \sqrt{S_{\delta C}}=2C_0\sqrt{S_y}. \]

Step C: sideband voltage from capacitance fluctuation

Using carrier angular frequency \(\omega_0=2\pi f_0\), carrier RMS current \(I_0\), and sideband offset \(\delta\omega\), with sideband convention \([V_{USB},V_{LSB}^*]\):

\[ V_{USB}= i\,\frac{I_0}{2\omega_0 C_0^2}\,\delta C_{\delta\omega}, \qquad V_{LSB}^{*}= -i\,\frac{I_0}{2\omega_0 C_0^2}\,\delta C_{\delta\omega}. \]

So the source is fully correlated and phase-like (antisymmetric sidebands).

Step D: equivalent USB/LSB voltage PSD

\[ S_{V,USB}=S_{V,LSB} =\frac{I_0^2}{4\omega_0^2C_0^4}S_{\delta C} =\frac{I_0^2}{\omega_0^2C_0^2}S_y. \]

Equivalent ASD form:

\[ \sqrt{S_{V,USB}}=\sqrt{S_{V,LSB}} =\frac{I_0}{\omega_0 C_0}\sqrt{S_y} =V_0\sqrt{S_y}, \]

where \(V_0=I_0/(\omega_0 C_0)\) is the carrier RMS capacitor voltage.
