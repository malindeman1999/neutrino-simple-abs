Kapitza Coupling Channel

Phonon-in-Ho to phonon-in-membrane interface coupling estimate at 100 mK.

Channel Definition

For phonon transfer across the Ho-membrane interface, use:

\[ \tau_{K} \approx \frac{C_{mem}}{G_{K}}, \]

where \(G_K\) is the Kapitza (thermal boundary) conductance between Ho-side phonons and membrane phonons.

Kapitza Model

Use the standard low-temperature interface scaling:

\[ G_K = A_{int}\,h_K\,T^3, \]

with interface area \(A_{int}\), coefficient \(h_K\) (material-pair dependent), and temperature \(T\).

Assumptions and Numbers at 100 mK

ParameterValue
Membrane heat capacity\(C_{mem}\approx 1.15\times10^{-16}\,\mathrm{J/K}\) (0.72 eV/mK)
Interface area\(A_{int}\approx 10\times10\,\mu\mathrm{m}^2 = 1\times10^{-10}\,\mathrm{m^2}\)
\(h_K\) range (first pass)\(5\times10^3\) to \(5\times10^4\,\mathrm{W\,m^{-2}\,K^{-4}}\)
Temperature\(T=0.1\,\mathrm{K}\)

Then:

\[ G_K \approx A_{int}h_KT^3 \approx 5\times10^{-10}\text{ to }5\times10^{-9}\,\mathrm{W/K}. \]

and

\[ \tau_K \approx \frac{C_{mem}}{G_K} \approx 0.023\text{ to }0.23\,\mu\mathrm{s}. \]

Interpretation

This channel is typically fast for the assumed contact area, but can become slower if effective interface area is reduced, interface quality is poor, or there is additional athermal bottlenecking near the interface.
